About Me

I am a junior at The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill majoring in multimedia journalism and political science.

I am currently interning as a reporter for The Chapel Hill News, a subsidiary of The News & Observer in Raleigh.

I work for the Investigations Team, Multimedia Desk, and Graphics Desk at The Daily Tar Heel, UNC’s independent, student-run newspaper. I produce videos, take photos, design pages, and create graphics for print and online.

Previous Experience

Last semester I was an assistant editor on the City Desk for The Daily Tar Heel. My regular beats included development policy and local government.

I was the university desk editor for The Daily Tar Heel last summer. In addition to coordinating a team of writers, I wrote stories about the NCAA’s sanctions against UNC’s athletic programs, UNC’s academic probation following their accreditation review, the removal of a KKK leader’s name from a campus building, and vandalism of a confederate monument on campus.

I also served as the editor-in-chief of Southern Neighbor magazine, a regional, monthly publication run by students, from June through September 2015.

Last year I was an assistant editor for the university desk at The Daily Tar Heel. I covered student activism, institutional racism, and the academic-athletic scandal at UNC.

I spent my first year at Elon University, where I was the assistant news editor for the student newspaper, The Pendulum. I freelanced for The Poynter Institute shortly afterward.
